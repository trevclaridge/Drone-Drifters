# How to Build a Drone

## Class

The first step in building a drone from scratch is to pick your new drone's class. The class acts as a basic chassis upon which you will layer a multitude of upgrades and customizations to make your new drone work for your needs.

There are 12 drone classes to choose from:

1. **Fighter** - The primary and most basic combat drone class. Quick, effective, and cheap to build.
2. **Cruiser** - A slightly upgraded combat drone class. A tad slower than a Fighter, but a little beefier and highly versatile.
3. **Destroyer** - The largest and most robust of the combat drone classes. Having a Destroyer nearby just seems to make every attack more effective.
4. **Carrier** - The drone class with space to spare. A Carrier drone can be a life preserver for those broken drones listing about, barely keeping the lights on. Dock up in one of its many roomy drone bays, slap on some duct tape, then get back out there.
5. **Artillery** - The long-range specialist drone class. Good for drones you want to keep on the outside and generally out of the line of fire.
6. **Survey** - While initialized designed for scientific pursuits, the Survey drone class has more than proven its utility in combat. A scan from a Survey drone can identify exactly where to put a shot to cause the most carnage.
7. **Tender** - The drone class focused entirely on repairing other drones in the haze of battle. Tender drones ought to be constructed with bright glowing halos for how much we all rely on them.
8. **Miner** - This drone cannot be rocked, and/or rolled. With robust stabilization and feedback limiters, Miner drones don't tend to feel those blasts meant to suppress a big area of the theater of battle.
9. **Hauler** - This drone class is all about moving stuff around. If you need it moved, then you need a Hauler, even if that "it" is another drone.
10. **Personnel Transport** - A drone class essentially yelling "All aboard!" for all nearby Drifter consciousnesses. Originally designed for short ranged passenger travel, Personnel Transport drones also have huge utility in acting as a home base for Drifter minds needing somewhere to be fast.
11. **Luxury Liner** - Every amenity your little heart could desire can be found on drones of this class. While not especially useful by itself in a battle, you'll likely be spending a lot of time here in your off hours.
12. **Colony Seed** - A drone class looking forward to the great tomorrow. Colony Seed drones are full of prospective colonists looking for a new place to call home. This is where you'll find the people you're fighting for.

Your base of operations will likely be a Luxury Liner or Colony Seed. It'll be your mothership, your post, your home, and, especially important, where your bodies will be when you're out there throwing your consciousness around and blowing things up. If your mothership breaks, your body breaks, too; try not to let that happen.

## Systems

After picking your drone's class, you'll equip it with some systems, modules that give it the functionality and firepower it needs to protect the mothership.
