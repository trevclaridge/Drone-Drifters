# Enemy Turns

Enemies act very similarly to Drifters in combat, but there are some key differences that will be discussed here.

The biggest difference is that all enemies are powered by one standard pool, that of the GM. The GM rolls and uses a standard pool just as Drifters do, but **their standard pool can be allocated to any system on any enemy combatant that they control**; the GM does not have to move a drifted consciousness between drones/ships to activate systems, unless specified by that particular enemy's statblock.

Another difference is the structure of the GM's standard pool. Instead of the generic 4 d6s, **a GM should build their standard pool with 2 d6s for each Drifter in the combat**. A good practice is to keep these pairs of d6s separate from each other, and try to use each pair to attack a different Drifter. This spreads the damage and puts pressure on each Drifter individually, keeping them engaged. If a Drifter can't be targeted for whatever, reason, its okay to use "their" dice to target a different Drifter.

Important to note here also is that, as the GM's fleet of enemies begins to dwindle and ships are destroyed in battle, the GM will have more die pips to allocate to individual enemies. This keeps the combat interesting, as **bigger and bigger attacks occur the longer combat prolongs**.
